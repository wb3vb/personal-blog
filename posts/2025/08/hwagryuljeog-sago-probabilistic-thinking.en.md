****​**Even investing logically and diligently can lead to losses.**

### Conversely, illogical investments like meme coins can also lead to profits.

That said, I recognize that the former has a higher **"probability"** of turning a profit, and if the results accumulate, logical investing is bound to come out ahead over the long run.

​The macro view is the "input" layer of investing.
Value investing is a complete investment strategy, a philosophy, and a decision-making process.
An "end-to-end" process of estimating an asset's fair value and, if it is undervalued beyond the margin of safety, buying and then managing it.

​How you analyze the macro and what action you take differ according to your own investment philosophy and strategy.
So the point is that an output emerges only when there is a "frame" set up in response to the input.

​Input → frame → output
Information gathered from various media → the information-processing engine called the brain → investment decisions

​However, if this frame is not established, investment judgment is difficult.
\*Investing is the product of quiet labor built up day after day.

## **An Example of the Investment Process**

**1. Comparing the Characteristics of Asset Classes, Products, and ETFs**

Comparing gold and gold-miner ETFs
The baseline assumption going in is that gold-miner stocks are a lousy investment.
→ While gold returned 52% after COVID, the gold-miner ETF (ticker: GDXJ) managed only a 9% return
→ On a 15-year basis, while gold rose 111%, the gold-miner ETF recorded -42%
→ Gold miners have a variety of problems, such as budget overruns, failures in cost management, embezzlement, and equity dilution through paid-in capital increases.

## **2. Establishing the Rationale from the Characteristics Comparison**

Gold-miner ETFs show "superior returns" when the price of gold surges.
However, as with the reasoning above, if the price of gold is flat or falling, the price actually melts away.
ex) Early 2016, gold +30% → gold miners +155%
ex) End of 2018, gold +70% → gold miners +118%
ex) Recently, while gold rose +18%, the gold-miner ETF (GDXJ) rose 32%

The reason? Because the fixed-cost ratio is high, if they can't sell it's a big loss, but once they clear the break-even point, most of the revenue becomes profit margin.
This is because the costs of gold miners are mostly "fixed costs."
Whether they sell gold or not, whether the price of gold is high or low → CAPEX and capital expenditure on exploration, development, and infrastructure are poured in on a fixed basis.
Therefore, when the price of gold surges → enormous profits/dividends increase → the stock price skyrockets.
+ On top of that, since they are undervalued when gold is flat, the rise in a bull market appears explosive.

## **3. Overall Macro Analysis**

From Q3, Yellen steadily sprays fiscal spending all the way to the presidential election → inflation returns → the potential for gold prices to rise emerges.
Therefore, I judge that gold miners can produce a corresponding rise → and among them, I see "gold miners" as having slightly more upside than gold.
That said, I keep as a baseline that gold miners are not a good investment in normal times.
(\* High potential returns come with high risk.)​

## **4. Stock Price Direction by Scenario**

### Best-Case Scenario for the U.S. Stock Market (Gradual Rise)

1\) A very mild recession arrives, and inflation and long-term interest rates come under downward pressure.
2) Yellen carries out fiscal spending that moderately supports growth, but only to a degree that does not bring on inflation.
3) The Fed provides covering fire through monetary policy so that long-term rates do not spike because of the fiscal spending.

### Worst-Case Scenario for the U.S. Stock Market (Crash)

1\) Yellen pumps in liquidity, and inflation reignites.
2) Long-term interest rates rise due to an oversupply of Treasuries.
3) For the sake of reelection, Biden cracks down on illegal immigrants, and through immigration policy and the like, the unemployment rate rises and retail sales decline.
4) The Fed, hemmed in by inflation, finds it hard to ease monetary policy and wavers back and forth.

### So What?

- Best-case scenario → gold/gold miners can mostly do well; at worst, they stay around flat.
- Worst-case scenario → the environment becomes very favorable for gold/gold miners.

​A liquidity party through fiscal spending & stagflation has generally brought about surges in gold and silver.
Therefore, a high-expected-value situation is one where an environment for gold to leap, along with favorable supply-and-demand conditions, can take shape.

## **5. Building a Portfolio from the Conclusion**

In conclusion, gold or gold-miner ETFs can deliver a slight softening when U.S. stocks do well, and a sharp surge when they do poorly.
Taking into account the macro environment that will unfold going forward, from an investment perspective one can build an attractive portfolio by purchasing gold-miner ETFs.

​No one knows how the macro will play out.

However, when we weight each of the probabilities, we can make the investment choice with the currently higher expected value and raise our investment multiplier.​
